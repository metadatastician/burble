# 13. Embeddability, dogfood-first adoption & the community participation escalator

Date: 2026-07-08

## Status

Proposed (first draft)

## Context

Embeddability is the *delivery half* of the founding thesis (ADR-0012): the
AI-assisted connection & support only matters if it arrives **inside** the
game / launcher / meeting, not as yet-another-app to configure. It is
therefore the **primary lever for adoption**.

But adoption tools get exactly **one first impression.** A dev drops the embed
in and within ninety seconds decides "lovely" or "janky, forget it" — and if
janky, they *tell people*. Shipping a "final" drop-in before it is proven is
the most expensive possible version of the "half-measure" mistake (ADR-0011 /
Q6), because it is made at the point of maximum visibility.

Separately, one person cannot build the whole vision (owner: "I'm just one
person"). The answer, reached repeatedly today, is to scale the *invitation*
rather than the person — and there is a ready-made structure for it: **TPCF
(the Tri-Perimeter Contribution Framework)**, created by the owner for
`betlang`, never yet adopted for Burble.

## Decision

### 1. Embeddability is the primary adoption lever

The drop-in that puts Burble (voice + AI-assist per ADR-0012, on the E2EE/edge
substrate per ADR-0010/0011) *inside* a game/launcher/app is the front door.
See the embeddability work (task: drop-in client module, `Burble.join`-style
minimal API).

### 2. Dogfood-first — never ship the front door untested

The embed is proven and hardened in the owner's **own estate games first**
(IDApTIK, Slavia, the game-as-data work) — real embeds, real friction, found
and fixed where nobody is watching — **before any public "final" release.**
Only when the owner would happily ship their *own* game on it does it earn a
public "drop this in." Consistent with the estate `dogfood-gate` and the
no-half-measure honesty line: the stakes are highest here because embed *is*
the adoption play.

### 3. Adopt TPCF as the participation escalator

Adopt TPCF's three concentric perimeters — *Inner Sanctum* (core), *Middle
Ring* (library), *Outer Circle* (community) — with their graduation paths,
mapped to Burble's structure. See the companion `docs/TPCF-BURBLE.adoc`.

### 4. Add a wish-capture floor *below* the outer circle

TPCF's outer circle still assumes "open a PR." Burble adds a rung beneath it:
the **feedback-o-tron** wish-capture floor. Anyone — a 7-year-old — *speaks or
types a wish* ("make Anya's hair blond," "the submarine looks rubbish, can I
use my own picture?"), with **no code, no PR, no git**, and the escalator
carries the wish *up* to whoever can grant it. This is **intent over settings
(ADR-0011 principle), applied to contribution**: you don't need to know *how*
to contribute; you say what you wish, and the system routes it. The floor
reaches the ground.

### 5. Gamify the escalator (in time)

Perimeters become levels; wishes and fixes become quests; graduating a
contribution outer → middle → inner is *levelling up*. Fitting, in a project
about play. Participation is designed as the game, not bolted on.

### 6. The range is the point: child's wish → proof-theorist's proof

The *same* framework welcomes the blond-hair wish **and** a formal verification
of a core guarantee (adversarial testing, proof-theoretic rigour). One
escalator, from "make it blond" to large countable ordinals. That radical
inclusivity — a seven-year-old and a formal-methods researcher on the same
rails — is the differentiator.

## Relationship to other decisions

* **ADR-0012 (founding thesis)** — embed is its delivery half; the escalator is
  *how the thesis gets built by everyone*, at every level.
* **ADR-0010 / ADR-0011** — the Inner Sanctum is the trustworthy substrate
  (E2EE/edge/presence); core changes must never weaken the seal, hence the
  strictest perimeter.
* **feedback-o-tron** — the estate tool that is the wish-capture floor.
* **dogfood-gate** — the estate CI value that decision 2 formalises for embed.
* **"I'm just one person"** — the escalator is the systematised answer: scale
  the invitation, not the person.

## Consequences

### Positive
* An adoption moat: radically inclusive participation (child → researcher),
  with the game itself as the contribution surface (players → participants).
* Dogfood-first protects the single first impression adoption depends on.

### Negative / caveats
* Dogfood-first is slower to public release — deliberately; the alternative
  (ship janky, get branded "pants") is fatal, not merely slow.
* The wish-capture floor needs triage/routing so wishes don't pile up unheard
  (a dead feedback box is worse than none).
* Gamification must reward genuine contribution, not gaming-the-metric.

# 12. The founding thesis: AI-assisted connection & support (why Burble exists)

Date: 2026-07-08

## Status

Proposed (first draft — the motivating stories and thesis, to be refined by
the owner)

## Context — the problem Burble exists to solve

Burble did not begin as "a voice app." It began from a specific, repeated,
human pain: **getting people connected, configured, and helped — together —
is so hard that it excludes people, ruins the moment, damages relationships,
and dumps an impossible load onto whoever in the room is most technical or
most vulnerable.** The tool then steps back as if none of that is its fault.

Three lived scenarios, each sharper than the last:

=== 1. Remote support — "just give me your access, this is insane"

You want to help someone, or collaborate, or demo. Instead: "install these
thirty things first," "turn on these dangerous settings," "just give me your
remote access." The forward-deployed engineer a company has to *send* purely
to make adoption happen. All the friction lives between two people who just
wanted to do a thing together.

=== 2. Game night — sorted before the fun dies

Friends gather to play. Then: whose server, which mod versions, port
forwarding, "can you hear me," "why can't I connect to you." Forty minutes
later either it's too late to play, or someone had to leave, or everyone's so
worn down that the *mood is dead* — the very thing they came for, poisoned by
the setup. Worst case: they give up and don't play at all. The difficulty
arrives at exactly the wrong moment — when everyone is excited and ready — and
drains the excitement out of the room.

=== 3. Accessibility — sorted so no one is shut out

A meeting starts. A disabled woman asks, "did you switch accessibility on?"
She tries to talk someone through a menu she can't see and he can't find. It's
buried among a thousand settings, on the wrong account, so the only fix is
"let's start over with mine" — which nobody does. And the cost is not lost
time; it is a **person excluded from participating**, while the meeting rolls
on without her.

And the wreckage the tool manufactures around that one missing setting:

* the technologist is shamed — "call yourself a technologist" — while doing
  three other things at once, the single point of failure expected to know
  a thousand settings;
* the disabled person is resented for "holding things up" *and* hurt, because
  the fumbling reads as *not caring*;
* relationships take damage; the deal walks out the door;

— when **nobody did anything wrong.** The software created a situation where
everyone loses and everyone blames each other.

## Decision — the thesis

Burble is an **AI-assisted connection & support layer**: an AI, **embedded**
and **present on every side**, that turns *intent* into the right connection
and the right configuration, in real time — so that **the tool absorbs the
load instead of dumping it on a human.** Nobody has to be the hero, and nobody
has to be the scapegoat.

Everything else Burble does — E2EE, presence, edge-cleverness, voice — is the
**trustworthy substrate**. *This* is the point that sits on top of it.

### The four mechanisms

1. **AI-assist on every side.** Each participant's AI can *see* the connection
   and configuration state and act on it — walking a human through it,
   diagnosing failures ("UDP 7373 is blocked — here's the one-liner"),
   reconciling settings across everyone, and coordinating the two sides with
   each other. Built on the **P2P AI bridge** (Claude-to-Claude over the data
   channel) — already the single most-proven part of the codebase, and the
   original seed of the whole idea.

2. **Intent over settings.** You never hunt a menu. You *say what you need* —
   "I need captions," "make this work with my screen reader," "I can't hear
   well," "get us connected" — and the AI turns on the right things **on the
   right side**, live. No "whose account, let's restart." Configuration by
   conversation, not by menu archaeology. This is the direct antidote to the
   thousand-settings problem.

3. **Drop-in embeddability.** For the help to arrive *in time*, it must live
   inside the game / the launcher / the meeting / the moment — not in
   yet-another-app you also have to configure. A separate tool you must set up
   is just more of the friction we are trying to kill. (This is the delivery
   half of the thesis; see the embeddability work.)

4. **On the trustworthy substrate.** The AI absorbs the load *without*
   becoming a surveillance layer: E2EE and edge-first (ADR-0011), presence and
   trust zones (ADR-0010), identity that grows with you. The AI does the
   sorting; it never becomes the thing that watches you.

### How the thesis dissolves the wreckage (scenario 3, precisely)

* The AI is the tech person, so the human is no longer the single point of
  failure doing three things at once.
* The disabled participant's need is stated as intent and sorted instantly, on
  the correct side — she is included from the first second, never the
  bottleneck.
* There is no fumbling to misread as "not caring" — the caring is built into
  the tool doing the work quietly and immediately.
* The relationships and the deal survive, because the friction never gets the
  thirty seconds it needs to turn people against each other.

## Relationship to other decisions

* **Q5 (LLM.Transport)** — the "reach an LLM" plumbing for mechanism 1,
  including the seam for a local / on-device assist LLM (privacy-preserving,
  per the ADR-0011 degradation-ladder ethos). *Keep and wire it* — it is not
  optional infrastructure, it is the founding feature's spine.
* **Q7 (embeddability)** — mechanism 3, the delivery half. Q5 and Q7 are the
  two halves of this one thesis; the game-night scenario is where they meet.
* **ADR-0010 / ADR-0011** — the trustworthy substrate (mechanism 4).
* **P2P AI bridge** (`client/web/burble-ai-bridge.js`) — the existing,
  most-proven seed of mechanism 1.

## Consequences

### Positive
* A genuine differentiator/moat: AI-mediated connection-and-configuration by
  intent, on every side, embedded — nobody does this.
* Moves the load off humans; protects the excluded, the overloaded, and the
  moment itself.

### Negative / caveats
* Ambitious; must be built incrementally on the proven bridge.
* The AI acting on a user's behalf must be bounded and consent-gated so
  "the AI sorts it" never becomes "the AI does something you didn't want."

## Next increment (concrete first step)

**Give the AI eyes on the connection.** Emit connection/configuration state as
structured events the AI bridge can read — join started, ICE gathering,
connected, *failed + why* (symmetric NAT, blocked port, bridge offline),
plus accessibility/config intents. That single step turns the proven
"two Claudes can talk" into "the AI can *see* what's wrong and help fix it,"
and everything else in the thesis builds from there.
